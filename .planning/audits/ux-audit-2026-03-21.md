- Most aircraft aligned within 20° of airport bearing ✅

- Approach speeds: 161-180 kts (realistic for commercial jets on approach) ✅
- No FAA 250kt violations below FL100 ✅
- Altitude decreases with distance — proper glide slope ✅

### Departure Lifecycle — ISSUE FOUND

| Metric | Value | Expected |
|--------|-------|----------|
| Climbing flights | **0** | 5-15 |
| Departures in "cruising" | 10 | <5 initially |
| Min departure cruising altitude | 28,587 ft | Should see some at 5,000-15,000 ft climbing |

**Issue:** No aircraft ever enter the `climbing` phase. Departures transition directly from `ground` → `cruising` at high altitude. This means:
- The "Climbing" color (green) in the header legend never appears
- No visual distinction between a just-departed aircraft and one at cruise
- Unrealistic — should see climbing aircraft near the airport with increasing altitude

**Root cause:** The fallback flight generator likely doesn't implement a gradual climb phase. When a ground aircraft departs, it's spawned at cruising altitude with cruising phase immediately.

## 9. Recommended Next Actions

### Immediate (high impact, moderate effort)
1. **Add climbing phase** — This is the most visible realism gap. A departing aircraft should climb from 0→35,000ft over ~15 minutes, visible as green dots near the airport.
2. **Rebalance departure ratio** — Generate closer to 50/50 arrivals/departures.
3. **Add EZY to airline lookup** — Quick fix for Geneva's second-largest carrier showing 0%.

### Medium-term
4. **Expose detailed phases to frontend** — Let users distinguish "taxiing" from "parked", "on final" from "descending enroute".
5. **Merge WebSocket connections** — Single shared WS reduces resource usage and eliminates reconnect divergence.
6. **Add origin/destination to flight list** — Most useful information for ATC-style overview.
