# UX Review — Airport Digital Twin

**Date:** 2026-03-21
**Reviewer:** Claude (acting as UX designer)
**App URL:** https://airport-digital-twin-dev-7474645572615955.aws.databricksapps.com
**Build:** v0.1.0, commit #274
**Airports tested:** KSFO (San Francisco), EGLL (London Heathrow), RJTT (Tokyo Haneda)

---

## Executive Summary

The Airport Digital Twin is a visually impressive real-time simulation with strong fundamentals: accurate OSM-based airport layouts, realistic airline mixes per airport, rich flight detail panels, and a working turnaround/baggage system. However, several issues significantly undermine the demo's credibility as a digital twin: **flights never depart** (no climbing phase observed), the **FIDS is disconnected from live data**, and **3D aircraft are too dark to identify**. These are the three highest-priority fixes.

**Overall UX Score: 7/10** — Excellent visual foundation, but lifecycle gaps and data disconnects prevent it from feeling like a live, coherent simulation.

---

## 1. 2D View

### What Works Well
- **OSM overlay accuracy:** Terminal buildings, taxiways, aprons, and runways align precisely with satellite imagery. Tested at KSFO, EGLL, and RJTT.
- **Aircraft silhouette sizing:** At max zoom, aircraft silhouettes are proportionally correct relative to terminal buildings. They match real satellite imagery scale.
- **Gate labels:** Clear, readable gate numbers placed at correct positions along terminal buildings.
- **Flight detail panel:** Rich and informative — shows callsign, ICAO hex, state, origin/destination, aircraft type, position, speed, heading, vertical rate, delay prediction with confidence %, turnaround progress, baggage status, and active GSE. This is excellent.
- **Trajectory visualization:** Approach paths curve in from the origin bearing direction (e.g., MCO approach to KSFO comes from the southeast). Departure trajectories aim toward the destination. The polyline rendering with altitude-coloring is intuitive.
- **Airline mix realism:** RJTT shows ~45 ANA, ~38 JAL, ~8 SKY, ~5 SFJ, ~5 ADO, plus KAL and CES — accurate for Haneda. KSFO shows UAL, SWA, DAL, AAL, BAW — accurate for SFO. EGLL shows BAW dominance — accurate for Heathrow.
- **Satellite view toggle:** Clean switch between map tiles and satellite with OSM overlay preserved.

### Issues Found

| # | Severity | Issue | Details |
|---|----------|-------|---------|
| 2D-1 | **Medium** | No departure trajectory animation | Flights at gates with `origin_airport=HND` (departing) never visually push back, taxi, or take off. They sit at 0kts indefinitely. |
| 2D-2 | **Low** | Some gate-less ground flights | 6 flights on ground with no `assigned_gate` (e.g., JAL5846, ANA9628). They appear parked at arbitrary coordinates, not on a taxiway or runway. |
| 2D-3 | **Low** | Heading vs terminal wall | ADO8937 at Gate 16 has heading 200deg. Whether the nose truly faces the terminal wall depends on the gate's orientation — not verified for all gates, some may appear broadside to the building. |
| 2D-4 | **Info** | No runway activity visible | No aircraft shown on final approach roll-out or takeoff roll on the runway surface. Flights jump from descending (150kts, 200ft) directly to ground (0kts, 0ft) at the gate. |

## 2. 3D View

### What Works Well
- **Terminal buildings rendered as 3D blocks:** Correct footprints from OSM, extruded to reasonable heights.
- **Aircraft 3D models present:** Recognizable silhouettes at gates. Multiple model types visible (A320, B737, etc.).
- **Camera controls work:** Orbit, zoom, pan, and "Reset View" button functional.
- **Loading spinner:** Airport switch shows overlay with spinner and progress bar — good feedback during load.

### Issues Found

| # | Severity | Issue | Details |
|---|----------|-------|---------|
| 3D-1 | **High** | Aircraft models too dark | 3D aircraft appear nearly black/silhouette against the terrain. Difficult to distinguish aircraft type or airline livery. Likely a lighting/material issue. |
| 3D-2 | **Medium** | Aircraft clustering at gates | Multiple aircraft appear stacked or overlapping at busy terminals, especially at close zoom. No collision avoidance for parked aircraft positions. |
| 3D-3 | **Medium** | No ground plane realism | Airport surface is a flat gray/green plane. No runway markings, taxiway lines, or apron texture. |
| 3D-4 | **Low** | Camera reset position too high | "Reset View" zooms out to a very high altitude, making aircraft invisible. A mid-level terminal view would be more useful as default. |
| 3D-5 | **Low** | No flight label in 3D | Unlike 2D view, 3D view doesn't show callsign labels on aircraft. Hard to identify which flight is which. |

## 5. Gate Operations & Flight Lifecycle

### What Works Well
- **Turnaround progress system:** 7-phase turnaround with percentage (Gate 16: "Unloading" 13% -> 17% -> 18% over ~4 minutes). Phases advance in real-time.
- **Active GSE tracking:** Shows belt loaders, ground power, etc. per gate.
- **Baggage status:** Detailed bag counts — total, delivered, connecting, at-risk bags with reason ("Tight connection time").
- **Delay prediction:** ML-based delay forecast with confidence % (e.g., "Slight Delay +11min, 85%").
- **Gate occupancy tracking:** Real-time available/occupied counts per terminal that update as flights arrive.
- **Realistic routes:** Domestic flights (ITM, CTS, FUK, OKA) and international (PVG, TPE, ICN, SIN, LAX) from RJTT.

### Issues Found — CRITICAL

| # | Severity | Issue | Details |
|---|----------|-------|---------|
| GL-1 | **Critical** | Flights never depart | Over 5+ minutes of observation: 0 climbing flights at any point. State distribution only shifts cruising -> descending -> ground. Flights accumulate at gates (41 -> 49 ground, 51 -> 43 cruising). No pushback, taxi-out, or takeoff exists in the simulation. |
| GL-2 | **Critical** | FIDS disconnected from live data | FIDS shows US airlines (Delta, Southwest, United, American) with US gate codes (B15, D4, F22) at Tokyo Haneda. It generates its own synthetic data independently from the actual simulation. Airlines, gates, and routes don't match the live flight list. |
| GL-3 | **High** | No taxi animation | Flights descending at 150kts/200ft instantly appear parked at gate at 0kts. No taxi-in phase. The 2 taxiing flights observed (JAL8794, ANA6936 at 25kts) appeared briefly but without visible movement along taxiway paths. |
| GL-4 | **Medium** | Gate-less flights parked randomly | 6 flights on ground with `assigned_gate: null`. These appear at arbitrary map coordinates, not on taxiways or runways. 0 velocity — appear stranded. |
| GL-5 | **Medium** | Turnaround rate not calibrated to departure time | ADO8937 at 13% -> 18% in 4 minutes. At this rate, 100% takes ~90 minutes. Est departure was 36 minutes away. The turnaround clock isn't calibrated to estimated departure time. |

## 7. Improvement Plan (Prioritized)

### P0 — Critical (Demo Blockers)

#### 1. Implement full departure lifecycle
- Add climbing flight phase: turnaround complete -> pushback -> taxi-out -> takeoff roll -> climb
- When turnaround reaches 100%, transition flight to taxi state with velocity
- Animate taxi along OSM taxiway paths to runway
- Generate climbing trajectory toward destination bearing
- Recycle the flight slot: once out of view, respawn as a new inbound flight
- **Why:** Without this, the simulation is a one-way funnel that fills up gates and stalls. This is the #1 demo blocker.
- **Effort:** Large (backend flight state machine + frontend animation)

#### 2. Connect FIDS to live flight data
- FIDS arrivals: show the actual descending/arriving flights from the simulation
- FIDS departures: show ground flights with turnaround progress and est_departure
- Use correct airport's airline names, gate codes, and routes
- **Why:** FIDS is a natural showcase feature for demos. Currently it shows completely fabricated data that contradicts what the user sees on the map.
- **Effort:** Medium (wire existing flight data into FIDS component)

### P1 — High (Visual Quality)

#### 3. Fix 3D aircraft lighting/materials
- Add ambient + directional lights to the 3D scene
- Apply proper material colors/textures to aircraft models
- Consider basic airline color schemes (ANA blue, JAL red)
- **Effort:** Small-Medium

#### 4. Add taxi animation on ground
- Show aircraft moving along taxiway paths (from OSM data) at 15-25kts
- Both for arrivals (runway -> gate) and departures (gate -> runway)
- Use taxiway polylines for routing (A* pathfinding)
- **Effort:** Large

#### 5. Add runway operations
- Show aircraft on final approach over the runway (below 200ft)
- Show takeoff roll acceleration on runway
- Brief runway occupancy visualization
- **Effort:** Medium

### P2 — Medium (Polish)

#### 6. Sync 2D/3D selection and camera
- Clicking a flight in 2D should highlight it when switching to 3D
- Optionally sync zoom area between views
- **Effort:** Medium

#### 7. Reduce airport switch time
- Pre-cache next airport's OSM data in background
- Show incremental loading (map first, then flights)
- Target: <5s switch time for cached airports
- **Effort:** Medium

#### 8. Calibrate turnaround timing to departure
- Turnaround progress should align with est_departure time
- If departure is in 36 minutes and progress is 13%, rate should accelerate
- Each of 7 phases should have realistic durations (deboarding 10-20min, refueling 15-25min, etc.)
- **Effort:** Small

#### 9. Fix gate-less ground flights
- Flights on ground with no gate should be positioned on a taxiway
- Give them taxi velocity or assign to a gate
- Never show a parked aircraft at a random coordinate
- **Effort:** Small

#### 10. 3D ground plane improvement
- Add runway markings and taxiway lines to 3D view
- Basic apron/concrete texture
- Import runway polygons from OSM
- **Effort:** Medium

### P3 — Low (Nice to Have)

11. **3D flight labels** — Show callsign labels on hover or toggle
12. **3D camera reset** — Default to mid-altitude terminal view instead of orbital height
13. **Airport switch progress details** — "Fetching OSM data... Generating flights... Building cache..."
14. **2D aircraft orientation at gates** — Ensure nose faces terminal wall using gate-to-building bearing

## 9. Raw Observations Log

### KSFO (San Francisco)
- 100 flights: UAL, SWA, DAL, AAL, BAW, FFT, ASA, JBU, SKW
- Gates: A-G terminals, correct SFO layout
- JBU5860: Gate C3, arrived from MCO, A320, trajectory shows SE approach
- FFT6478: Climbing to MFR, departure trajectory NNE — trajectory data exists but state never observed in later tests
- ASA8712: Descending from EUG, 4,800ft, 180kts approach

### EGLL (London Heathrow)
- 100 flights: BAW dominant (~60), plus VIR, EZY, UAE, SIA, DLH
- Large terminal complex with 4 terminals
- Successful switch from KSFO in ~18s

### RJTT (Tokyo Haneda)
- 100 flights: ANA ~45, JAL ~38, SKY ~8, SFJ ~5, ADO ~5, KAL ~1, CES ~2
- 9 terminals, 87 gates (Terminal 1 largest: 46 gates)
- Weather: 20C, 188@3kt, 10SM
- Over 5 minutes observation:
  - Cruising: 51 -> 43 (flights arriving)
  - Ground: 41 -> 49 (accumulating at gates)
  - Descending: 8 (steady inflow)
  - Climbing: 0 (constant — no departures ever observed)
  - 2 taxiing briefly (JAL8794, ANA6936 at 25kts)
  - ADO8937 turnaround: 13% -> 18% in 4 min (slowly advancing)
  - Gate occupancy: 57 -> 50 -> 44 available (steadily filling up)
